Intro to UX Design

Lecture 3

Description

UX design is about designing a complete experience for the user or customer without limiting it to a moment or touchpoint in time. For example, Japanese retailer Muji opened a pop-up apartment in London to give the users a great experience of their apparel and homeware collection. They organised workshops so people could learn more about how to use their products in their own environment. This entire concept had nothing to do with purchasing but focussed instead on creating an experience for the users.

Considering this, a whole experience includes all the moments of before, during, and after a specific function that a user aims to perform or see completed.

While designing, you should stretch your thinking beyond the moments of interaction with the users and consider their entire journey the interaction. This consideration of the overall user experience prepares you to develop a more profound empathy with them and goes beyond the narrow definitions. The more broadly you define the customer experience, the more opportunities you can identify for improvement.
